Website Visitors

Like most website operators, WNC Broadband Project collects non-personally-identifying information of the sort that web browsers and servers typically make available, such as the browser type, language preference, referring site, and the date and time of each visitor request. WNC Broadband Project's purpose in collecting non-personally identifying information is to better understand how WNC Broadband Project's visitors use its website. From time to time, WNC Broadband Project may release non-personally-identifying information in the aggregate, e.g., by publishing a report on trends in the usage of its website.

WNC Broadband Project also collects potentially personally-identifying information like Internet Protocol (IP) addresses for logged in users and for users leaving comments on https://wncbroadband.org/ blog posts. WNC Broadband Project only discloses logged in user and commenter IP addresses under the same circumstances that it uses and discloses personally-identifying information as described below.

Gathering of Personally-Identifying Information

Certain visitors to WNC Broadband Project's websites choose to interact with WNC Broadband Project in ways that require WNC Broadband Project to gather personally-identifying information. The amount and type of information that WNC Broadband Project gathers depends on the nature of the interaction. For example, we ask visitors who sign up for a blog at https://wncbroadband.org/ to provide a username and email address.

WNC Broadband Project may collect statistics about the behavior of visitors to its website. WNC Broadband Project may display this information publicly or provide it to others. However, WNC Broadband Project does not disclose your personally-identifying information.

Cookies

To enrich and perfect your online experience, WNC Broadband Project uses "Cookies", similar technologies and services provided by others to display personalized content, appropriate advertising and store your preferences on your computer.

A cookie is a string of information that a website stores on a visitor's computer, and that the visitor's browser provides to the website each time the visitor returns. WNC Broadband Project uses cookies to help WNC Broadband Project identify and track visitors, their usage of https://wncbroadband.org/, and their website access preferences. WNC Broadband Project visitors who do not wish to have cookies placed on their computers should set their browsers to refuse cookies before using WNC Broadband Project's websites, with the drawback that certain features of WNC Broadband Project's websites may not function properly without the aid of cookies.

By continuing to navigate our website without changing your cookie settings, you hereby acknowledge and agree to WNC Broadband Project's use of cookies.
