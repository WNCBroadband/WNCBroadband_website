WestNGN Resources

West Next Generation Network (WestNGN) is a collaborative effort hosted by Land of Sky Regional Planning Council (landofsky.org). WestNGN has operated in two stages, and a description of the two stages was made available to 2019 conference attendees (Land of Sky Regional Broadband Summary PDF).

Regional Resources

The Education and Research Consortium (ERC) is a non-profit organization that provides “middle mile” fiber networks to governmental and non-profit organizations in Western NC. Visit ercwnc.org

Southwestern Commission— a regional planning agency for the southwest corner of NC

  • 2018 Region A Broadband Assessment (View Assessment)
  • 2017 Broadband Summit Resources (Visit regiona.org)
  • Community Assessments And Broadband Profiles (View Slideshow)

The Appalachian Regional Commission (ARC) is an economic development agency federally chartered to assist Appalachian-defined states and counties, including many counties in western NC.

  • ARC Toolkit for Rural Communities Expand Broadband Service (View Article)
  • ARC’s report on their telecommunications initiative “Information Age Appalachia: A Rural Digital Divide Program” (Visit arc.gov's Search Link)

North Carolina State Resources

  • NC Dept. of Information Technology is charged with advancing a statewide plan for broadband. (Visit ncbroadband.gov)
  • “CONNECTING NORTH CAROLINA: State Broadband Plan” (View Report)
  • “Community Broadband Playbook: Building a Better Broadband infrastructure” (Visit ncbroadband.gov/playbook)
  • NC GREAT (Growing Rural Economies with Access to Technology) grants recently enacted by the state legislature to assist least served areas of the state. (Visit ncbroadband.gov/greatgrant)
  • NC League of Municipalities website on community efforts to acquire adequate broadband (Visit nclm.org)
  • “Leaping the Digital Divide,” 2019 white paper from NC League of Municipalities (View Report)
  • Institute for Local Reliance has identified broadband access as a priority. Their website provides updates on regional activities of interest. (Visit ilsr.org)
  • “North Carolina Connectivity: The Good, The Bad, and The Ugly, Institute for Local Self Reliance – 2016 report on broadband in NC in rural areas (View Report)
  • “NC Broadband Matters” - nice description of local resources and private/public partnerships (View Report)

National Resources

  • Community Networks Project – A project funded by foundations to assist communities with their own Internet infrastructure to promote economic prosperity and improve quality of life (Visit muninetworks.org)
  • Next Century Cities supports mayors and community leaders across the country as they seek to ensure that everyone has fast, affordable and reliable internet access. (Visit nextcenturycities.org)
  • The Coalition for Local Internet Choice – CLIC – represents a wide range of public and private interests who support the authority of local communities to make the broadband Internet choices that are essential for economic competitiveness, democratic discourse, and quality of life in the 21st century. (Visit localnetchoice.org)
  • Connect My Community- a non-profit organization assisting communties build broadband infrastructure (Visit connectmycommunity.org)
  • Broadband USA is hosted by The National Telecommunications and Information Administration’s (NTIA) promotes innovation and economic growth by supporting efforts to expand broadband. (Visit broadbandusa.ntia.doc.gov)
